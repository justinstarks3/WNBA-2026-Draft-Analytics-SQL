Methodology

How the draft scores and grades were computed.

Data Source

Statistics were scraped from ESPN's Women's College Basketball stats pages for the 2025–26 season (season=2026 parameter). Data covers 3,224 players across all NCAA Division I programs. This analysis is for educational and portfolio purposes only.

Per-40 Normalization

All volume stats (PTS, REB, AST, STL, BLK, TOV) are converted to per-40-minute rates to account for differences in playing time. A player averaging 12 points in 20 minutes is treated equivalently to a starter averaging 24 in 40 minutes.

Efficiency Metrics

eFG% = (FGM + 0.5×3PM) / FGA — weights 3-pointers appropriately.

TS% = PTS / (2 × (FGA + 0.44×FTA)) — true shooting accounts for free throws, the most complete efficiency metric.

Z-Score Standardization

Each metric is converted to a z-score (how many standard deviations above/below the class mean) so that completely different stats — points, steals, efficiency — can be combined fairly. Turnovers are sign-flipped (lower is better).

Draft Grades & Tiers

Grades are assigned by percentile rank within the draft class. Tier 1 (Franchise) = top 10%, Tier 2 (Lottery) = 75–90th percentile, Tier 3 (First Round) = 50–75th, Tier 4 (Second Round) = 25–50th, Tier 5 (Camp/Dev) = bottom 25%.

Limitations

This model does not account for: player age (WNBA eligibility requires turning 22 in draft year), competition level (Power 5 vs. mid-major), injury history, team role/usage, or any qualitative scouting factors. It is a quantitative baseline, not a complete draft guide.
