Overview

This dashboard analyzes 3,232 NCAAW prospects using a composite draft scoring model built entirely from box score statistics. Raw data was collected from ESPN's 2026 women's college basketball stats, then engineered into per-40 minute metrics and efficiency rates to normalize across different playing time levels.

⚠️ Data snapshot taken at approximately mid-season — stats and rankings will shift as the year progresses.

Metric Engineering

Raw box score stats are transformed into rate metrics that allow fair comparison regardless of playing time:

  • Per-40 stats — points, rebounds, assists, steals, blocks, turnovers scaled to 40 minutes
  • True Shooting % — PTS / (2 × (FGA + 0.44 × FTA))
  • Effective FG% — accounts for 3-point value
  • Stocks per 40 — (STL + BLK) per 40 minutes
  • AST/TOV ratio — playmaking efficiency

Z-Score Normalization

Each metric is converted to a z-score representing standard deviations above or below the draft class average. This allows combining completely different statistics (points, steals, efficiency) into a single fair composite score. Turnovers are negated (lower is better).

Draft Grades & Tiers

Grades are assigned based on percentile rank within the full draft class. Tiers correspond to draft round projections based on historical WNBA draft patterns.

Limitations

This model does not account for player age/eligibility, competition level differences between conferences, team role or usage context, injury history, or physical measurements. It is intended as a statistical starting point for prospect evaluation, not a definitive ranking.
